\chapter*{Abstract}
\addcontentsline{toc}{chapter}{Abstract}

This book introduces quantum information through executable experiments that begin
with classical bits and finite-sample probability and proceed to qubits, measurement,
quantum gates, phase, interference, two-qubit systems, entanglement, the Deutsch
algorithm, and simple models of noise. The intended readers are high-school students,
undergraduates, and general readers who know high-school mathematics but have not
studied quantum mechanics or linear algebra.

Each chapter follows a common sequence: an opening question, an experiment in the
companion learning app, an interpretation of the observed result, a step-by-step
mathematical explanation, a misconception check, and exercises. Optional sections
provide deeper linear-algebra and density-matrix treatments without interrupting the
main learning path.
